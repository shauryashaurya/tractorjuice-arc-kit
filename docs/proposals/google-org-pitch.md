## The Problem: Citizens Pay the Price for Broken Governance

Governments worldwide waste billions on failed technology programmes — and citizens bear the cost through delayed services, broken systems, and wasted public money. The root cause is consistent: fragmented, manual architecture governance.

- **Governance is effectively impossible for smaller agencies**: Comprehensive architecture governance requires large, specialist teams. Hundreds of smaller government agencies, arm's-length bodies, and local authorities — the organisations closest to citizens — simply cannot resource it. The result is that the services citizens depend on most have the weakest governance protecting them.
- **Manual processes consume weeks**: Creating requirements specifications, business cases, risk registers, and procurement documents takes **4-6 weeks per project** using traditional methods — meaning citizens wait months longer for digital services to reach delivery.
- **Compliance is aspirational, not achievable**: Each architect applies their own standards, templates, and formats. Typical compliance coverage against government frameworks (GDS Service Standard, Technology Code of Practice) sits at **40-60%** — leaving significant gaps that create risk for citizens and waste for taxpayers.
- **Failed projects waste public money**: UK NAO reports consistently identify poor architecture governance as a root cause of project overruns and failures. The UK alone spends **£20B+ annually** on technology; even a modest reduction in failure rates returns hundreds of millions to productive use.
- **Institutional knowledge walks out the door**: When architects leave, knowledge leaves with them. New staff start from scratch — repeating mistakes, duplicating work, and delaying delivery.

This is not just an efficiency problem. It is a structural barrier that determines which citizens get well-governed digital services and which do not.

### What It Does

ArcKit guides architects through the complete governance lifecycle:

1. **Project Planning** — Timeline, phases, and gates
2. **Architecture Principles** — Organisational standards and decision frameworks
3. **Stakeholder Analysis** — Drivers, goals, outcomes, and engagement strategies
4. **Risk Assessment** — HM Treasury Orange Book framework
5. **Business Case** — Green Book 5-case SOBC model
6. **Requirements** — Business, functional, non-functional, integration, and data requirements
7. **Data Modelling** — ERD, GDPR compliance, data governance
8. **Technology Research** — Build vs buy analysis with live web search
9. **Strategic Planning** — Wardley Mapping, roadmaps, strategy synthesis
10. **Vendor Procurement** — RFP/SOW, G-Cloud, DOS, evaluation frameworks
11. **Design Review** — HLD/DLD validation against principles and requirements
12. **Sprint Planning** — Backlog generation, user stories, Trello export
13. **Operations** — ServiceNow, DevOps, MLOps, FinOps
14. **Compliance** — GDS Service Standard, TCoP, Secure by Design, AI Playbook, ATRS, MOD JSP 936
15. **Reporting** — Project stories, MARP presentations
16. **Publishing** — GitHub Pages documentation sites

Every generated document follows a standardised template with document control, revision history, and full traceability from stakeholder goals through requirements to delivered outcomes.

## Alignment with Google AI Principles

ArcKit's design and governance approach align with Google's AI Principles:

- **Be socially beneficial**: Open-source toolkit improving public services for citizens
- **Avoid creating or reinforcing unfair bias**: Template-driven approach ensures consistent governance regardless of team size, experience, or department — democratising access
- **Be built and tested for safety**: Automated compliance checking against government security frameworks (NCSC CAF, Cyber Essentials)
- **Be accountable to people**: Full traceability chain from stakeholder goals through requirements to delivered outcomes; complete document control and audit trail
- **Incorporate privacy design principles**: Dedicated DPIA command, UK GDPR compliance built into data modelling
- **Uphold high standards of scientific excellence**: Structured, evidence-based governance methodology with measurable quality metrics
- **Be made available for uses that accord with these principles**: MIT-licensed, ensuring governments retain full control
