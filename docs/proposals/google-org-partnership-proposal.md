## Executive Summary

Government technology programmes fail at alarming rates — and fragmented, manual architecture governance is a root cause. But the problem is not just that governance is slow. For hundreds of smaller government agencies, arm's-length bodies, and local authorities, comprehensive architecture governance has been **effectively impossible** — they simply lack the 20-person architecture teams that large departments rely on. The result is that the organisations closest to citizens often have the weakest governance protecting the services those citizens depend on.

ArcKit changes what is possible. It is an open-source, AI-powered toolkit that enables a team of two or three architects to produce governance of the same depth and quality that previously required a department of twenty. Across 14+ UK Government domains — from NHS digital health to Cabinet Office AI strategy to Scottish Courts digital transformation — ArcKit has compressed 4-6 week governance processes to 3-5 days while simultaneously raising compliance coverage from a typical 40-60% to 90%+. The platform is production-ready (v2.8.5), MIT-licensed, and works across four major AI coding assistants.

This proposal seeks $1.5M-$2.5M to take ArcKit from proven case studies to systematic government infrastructure: piloting in 15+ departments, rigorously measuring impact, building training capacity, and preparing for international replication. In partnership with [Partner Organisation] — who bring [eligible status, government relationships, and evaluation expertise] — and with Google.org's support through the Accelerator programme, we aim to demonstrate that AI-assisted architecture governance can permanently democratise access to world-class governance for every government team, regardless of size.

## 3. Focus Area: Economy

### Why Economy?

Google.org's Economy focus area targets solutions that improve infrastructure, optimise public resources, and advance economic opportunity. Government technology governance sits at the centre of all three:

- **Infrastructure**: Government digital services *are* public infrastructure — appointment booking, tax filing, benefit claims, court access. Poor governance of the technology programmes that build this infrastructure directly degrades the infrastructure itself.
- **Resource optimisation**: Every government technology programme that fails or overruns wastes public money that could fund additional services. The UK alone spends £20B+ annually on technology; even a modest reduction in failure rates returns hundreds of millions to productive use.
- **Economic opportunity**: When government digital services work well, citizens and businesses interact with government faster and more efficiently — reducing administrative burden, accelerating decisions (patents, planning, benefits), and freeing economic activity that bureaucratic delay currently suppresses.

ArcKit's contribution to the Economy focus area is structural: by systematically improving the governance of government technology programmes, it protects public investment, accelerates service delivery, and ensures digital infrastructure is built to standard.

### 3.1 Problem Statement

Government technology programmes suffer from systemic governance failures that waste public money and delay the services citizens and businesses depend on.

**Scale of the Problem**:
- The UK Government spends over **£20 billion annually** on technology and digital services (HM Treasury Main Estimates)
- The NAO's *Digital Transformation in Government* report found that of 25 major government programmes examined, the majority suffered governance weaknesses that contributed to delays and cost overruns
- The NHS National Programme for IT — the largest civilian IT programme ever attempted — was abandoned at an estimated cost of **£10 billion**, with poor architecture governance cited as a contributing factor
- This is not unique to the UK: the US Government Accountability Office (GAO) maintains a High Risk List where IT programme management has appeared continuously since 2015, and the EU Court of Auditors has flagged similar governance failures in member state digitalisation programmes

**Root Causes**:
- **Manual governance processes**: Creating a requirements specification, business case, risk register, and procurement documentation takes **4-6 weeks per project** using traditional methods
- **Inconsistent standards**: Each department, programme, and architect applies different templates, formats, and quality thresholds
- **Fragmented tooling**: Architecture documents are scattered across Word, Confluence, PowerPoint, and SharePoint with no version control or traceability
- **Compliance burden**: UK Government mandates compliance with the Technology Code of Practice (13 points), GDS Service Standard (14 points), NCSC Cyber Assessment Framework, UK GDPR, and sector-specific frameworks — manual compliance checking is slow and incomplete
- **Knowledge loss**: When architects leave, institutional knowledge leaves with them; new staff must recreate governance artifacts from scratch

**Impact on Citizens**:
The downstream cost of governance failure is borne by citizens. When a government digital service is delayed by 12 months because requirements were incomplete, or a procurement is challenged because the evaluation framework was inconsistent, real people wait longer for real services. Consider:
- A patient who cannot book an NHS appointment online because the booking system was delivered without proper data protection impact assessment, triggering a post-launch suspension
- A small business waiting months for a patent decision because the IPO's case management system was procured without adequate requirements traceability, leading to vendor disputes
- A court user unable to access case information digitally because the Scottish Courts' technology strategy lacked the architectural governance to move from pilot to production

These are not hypothetical scenarios — they reflect the kinds of failures that ArcKit's case studies were designed to prevent. The link between governance quality and citizen outcomes is direct: **well-governed programmes deliver services on time, on budget, and to standard; poorly governed ones do not**.

## 4. Proposed Solution: ArcKit Platform

### 4.1 Core Capabilities

ArcKit covers the **complete enterprise architecture governance lifecycle** — from initial project planning through requirements, procurement, design review, compliance assessment, and documentation publishing. The platform provides 51 commands organised across 16 workflow phases:

| Phase | Commands | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| 0. Project Planning | `plan` | Timeline, phases, gates, Gantt charts |
| 1. Governance Foundation | `principles` | Enterprise architecture principles and standards |
| 2. Stakeholder Analysis | `stakeholders` | Drivers, goals, outcomes, engagement strategies |
| 3. Risk Assessment | `risk` | HM Treasury Orange Book framework |
| 4. Business Case | `sobc` | Green Book 5-case model (Strategic, Economic, Commercial, Financial, Management) |
| 5. Requirements | `requirements`, `data-model`, `dpia`, `platform-design`, `datascout`, `data-mesh-contract` | Business, functional, non-functional, data, GDPR |
| 6. Technology Research | `research`, `azure-research`, `aws-research`, `gcp-research` | Build vs buy, cloud service evaluation |
| 7. Strategic Planning | `wardley`, `roadmap`, `strategy`, `adr` | Wardley Mapping, roadmaps, ADRs |
| 8. Vendor Procurement | `sow`, `dos`, `gcloud-search`, `gcloud-clarify`, `evaluate` | RFP, G-Cloud, DOS, vendor evaluation |
| 9. Design Review | `hld-review`, `dld-review`, `diagram`, `dfd` | Architecture validation, diagrams |
| 10. Sprint Planning | `backlog`, `trello`, `story` | User stories, sprint planning, export |
| 11. Operations | `servicenow`, `devops`, `mlops`, `finops`, `operationalize` | Service management, CI/CD, cost governance |
| 12. Traceability | `traceability` | Requirements-to-design gap analysis |
| 13. Quality Assurance | `analyze`, `principles-compliance`, `health` | Governance quality scoring |
| 14. Compliance | `service-assessment`, `tcop`, `secure`, `ai-playbook`, `atrs`, `mod-secure`, `jsp-936` | GDS, TCoP, NCSC, AI, MOD |
| 15. Reporting | `story`, `presentation`, `pages` | Project narratives, slides, documentation sites |
| 16. Publishing | `pages` | GitHub Pages documentation portal |

### 4.2 How ArcKit's AI Works

ArcKit goes beyond simple AI prompting with an architecture designed for depth and reliability:

**5 AI Research Agents That Work Autonomously**:

When an architect needs to evaluate cloud services, research market options, or discover data sources, ArcKit deploys specialised AI agents that conduct the research independently — searching dozens of sources, comparing options, and returning structured findings. This mirrors how a human research analyst would work, but completes in minutes rather than days.

| Agent | What It Does | How It Works |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Market Research | Evaluates technology products, compares pricing, analyses build vs buy trade-offs | Searches the web autonomously, gathering vendor data and community reviews |
| Data Discovery | Finds external data sources (government open data, commercial APIs, datasets) | Searches UK Government portals, scores sources against project requirements |
| AWS Research | Evaluates Amazon Web Services for project needs | Queries authoritative AWS documentation in real-time |
| Azure Research | Evaluates Microsoft Azure services for project needs | Queries official Microsoft Learn documentation in real-time |
| GCP Research | Evaluates Google Cloud Platform services for project needs | Queries Google's developer documentation in real-time |

Each agent performs 20-50+ research queries autonomously before returning structured results — a depth of analysis that manual research cannot match in the same timeframe.

**Automated Quality Controls**:

ArcKit includes four automation hooks that enforce governance standards without manual intervention:
- **Project awareness**: Automatically detects existing artifacts and injects context so each new document builds on what already exists
- **Naming enforcement**: Auto-corrects filenames to standardised conventions, preventing the inconsistency that plagues manual governance
- **Compliance tool access**: Automatically connects to cloud provider documentation for authoritative, up-to-date reference material
- **Session intelligence**: Detects the project environment on startup and orients the architect immediately

**Real-Time Cloud Documentation Access**:

ArcKit connects directly to four authoritative cloud documentation systems, ensuring that architecture recommendations are based on current, official information rather than potentially outdated training data:
- AWS Knowledge (Amazon Web Services)
- Microsoft Learn (Azure)
- Google Developer Knowledge (Google Cloud Platform)
- DataCommons (public datasets and statistics)

## 9. Scalability and Sustainability

### 9.1 International Replication

ArcKit's architecture is designed for international scaling:

**Template-driven design**: Core governance patterns (stakeholder analysis, risk management, requirements engineering, vendor procurement) are universal. Jurisdiction-specific compliance modules are pluggable.

**Replication targets**:
| Priority | Jurisdictions | Compliance Frameworks |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Tier 1 | Five Eyes (US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand) | FedRAMP, DTA Digital Service Standard, NZISM |
| Tier 2 | EU member states | EU Digital Decade, eIDAS 2.0, DORA, AI Act |
| Tier 3 | Emerging digital governments | India (IndiaStack), Singapore (GovTech), UAE, Estonia (e-Governance) |

**Localisation approach**: Each new jurisdiction requires (a) compliance module development (~8-12 weeks), (b) template localisation, and (c) a local government pilot. The ArcKit core platform and 51 commands remain unchanged.

### 9.2 Open-Source Community Model

**Sustainability through community**:
- **Core maintainers**: ArcKit development team provides continuous platform development
- **Compliance contributors**: Government architects contribute jurisdiction-specific modules
- **Template contributors**: Organisations share customised templates for specific sectors
- **Training community**: Certified trainers deliver workshops independently

**Governance**: Adopt a lightweight open-source governance model (maintainer + contributors) with clear contribution guidelines, code of conduct, and release process.

### 9.3 Revenue Pathways (Post-Programme)

The programme builds towards financial sustainability through:

| Pathway | Description | Timeline |

| **Consulting services** | Partner organisation offers ArcKit deployment consulting | Year 2+ |
| **Custom compliance modules** | Paid development of niche compliance frameworks | Year 2+ |

The core platform remains free and open source. Revenue comes from value-added services around the platform.
